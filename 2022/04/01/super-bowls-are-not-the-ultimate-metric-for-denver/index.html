Super Bowls Are Not the Ultimate Metric For Denver

2022-04-01

The Denver Broncos have three Super Bowls, but one of the most prolific offenses in the 2013 team came up short in the playoffs. Which of these teams is the best Broncos team since the 1990 season?

The Denver Broncos have been consistently bad over the last six seasons, since hall of game quarterback and the undisputed GOAT Peyton Manning retired. Now armed with another hall of fame quarterback that has already established themselves as an elite player, it’s reminiscent of the trade for the equally undisputed GOAT John Elway. Will this acquisition be a repetition of history, or will it be another in a long line of quarterback busts? Only time will tell.

However, we should look to the past to determine which Broncos team was actually the best. They won the Super Bowl in 1997, 1998, and 2015, but the 2013 team had a dominant offense that shattered single-season records, however they fell short to the Seattle Seahawks in Super Bowl XLVIII. Or is there potentially another dark horse team that never reached the same level of success as these teams? Let’s find out. For the sake of simplicity, we’re going to focus only on Denver Broncos teams from last season back to 1990.

First we need to look at the offense for each of these teams in the context of the times. How did they compare to the other offenses in the league during the year they played?

To do this, I created a z-score for several statistics within each Denver team. I compared the total yards, total points, turnovers lost, yards per play, and total touchdowns. I then added all of the z-scores up to get a total offensive z-score, showing which teams were better than other teams in their respective years at gaining yards, scoring touchdowns, and not giving up the football.

That team excelled in every offensive category and were substantially better than all other offenses in the league that year

The 2013 team had one of the most prolific offenses of all time, averaging 460 yards, 38 points, and four and a half touchdowns per game. It was miles ahead of the even the next closest Broncos team, having a Z score nearly 3 points higher than the 1998 Super Bowl team.

However, as we know very well, offense is only half of the ball game. Unlike the Big-12 Conference, if you want to win in the NFL you need to have some resemblance of a defense. Many argue that defense is even more important than offense, as it’s easier to outscore an opponent who doesn’t score anything, than to outscore an opponent in a shootout. Just ask Patrick Mahomes about his time at Texas Tech.

The Simple Rating System takes a team’s average point margin and adjusts it up or down based on a postivie or negative strength of schedule. I took the offensive and defensive simple ratings for all of Denver teams to show both how well the offense and defense for each year performed.
