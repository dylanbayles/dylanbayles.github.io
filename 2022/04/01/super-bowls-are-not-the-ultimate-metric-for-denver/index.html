Super Bowls Are Not the Ultimate Metric For Denver

2022-04-01

The Denver Broncos have been consistently bad over the last six seasons, ever since hall of game quarterback and the undisputed GOAT Peyton Manning retired. However, they are a highly decorated team, having won three Super Bowls (tied for 7th most) and having appeared in eight of the big games (tied for 3rd most). Many argue as to whether Manning or John Elway was the best Broncos quarterback of all time, but I’m more interested in which team was the best.

They won the Super Bowl in 1997, 1998, and 2015, but the 2013 team had a dominant offense that shattered single-season records, however they fell short to the Seattle Seahawks in Super Bowl XLVIII. Or is there potentially another dark horse team that never reached the same level of success as these teams? Let’s find out. For the sake of simplicity, we’re going to focus only on Denver Broncos teams from last season dating back to 1990.

First we need to look at the offense for each of these teams in the context of the times. How did they compare to the other offenses in the league during the year they played? A z-score is a statistical measure of the distance from the mean that allows us to accomplish this.

I created a z-score for several offensive statistics within each Denver team. I compared the total yards, total points, turnovers lost, yards per play, and total touchdowns. I then added all of the z-scores up to get a total offensive z-score, showing which teams were better than other teams in their respective years at gaining yards, scoring touchdowns, and not giving up the football.

The 2013 team had one of the most prolific offenses of all time, averaging 460 yards, 38 points, and four and a half touchdowns per game. It was miles ahead of the even the next closest Broncos team, having a Z score nearly 3 points higher than the 1998 Super Bowl team.

However, as we know very well, offense is only half of the ball game. Unlike the Big-12 Conference, if you want to win in the NFL you need to have some resemblance of a defense. Many argue that defense is even more important than offense, as it’s easier to outscore an opponent who doesn’t score anything, than to outscore an opponent in a shootout. Just ask Patrick Mahomes about his time at Texas Tech.

The Simple Rating System takes a team’s average point margin and adjusts it up or down based on a positive or negative strength of schedule. I took the offensive and defensive simple ratings for all of Denver teams to show both how well both the offense and defense performed.

The only team out of our four in question that made the good offense, good defense section was the 1997 team. The 2015 team had astonishing defense, but the offense was sub par thanks to Manning’s neck being made of steel. The 1998 and 2013 teams had great offense, but the defense was not there to back it up.

Let’s take one closer look at the defensive stats to make the final call. I looked at the season rushing and passing yards of the opponents for our final four contenders to see how many of each type of yardage the defense was giving up.

It’s pretty apparent that without a defense, you’re going to get ran over by 35 points in the Super Bowl, regardless of how amazing your offense is. For that reason, the 2013 team is out of contention.

With the three Super Bowl teams remaining, the victor is clear. Even though the 2015 team had a slight advantage on the 1997 team in terms of defense, the offensive prowess was simply not there for the 2015 team. The 1998 team had a slightly better offense than the 1997 team based on the z-scores, but weren’t even above average for Denver teams in their defensive simple rating.

There’s plenty of crowns to be given out for the best offensive and defensive Broncos teams, but at the end of the day it doesn’t get you the best team award. You need to have a solid offense, a solid defense, and the GOAT quarterback to receive that award.

Hence, the 1997 team is the best Denver Broncos team of all time.
